## Note on the simulator-validation figure

The chart `figures/simulator_validation.png` displays **Pokémon card and attack names as text strings** (e.g. "Accompanying Flute", "Ball Roll", "Mirror Attack"). These are factual references to cards in the publicly-released Scarlet & Violet expansions, identical to how a deck list document references them. **No card art, no card image, no logo, no Pokémon character art is included.** Text references to cards are standard practice in TCG analysis (see "PTCG Live" export format) and are not the licensed asset.

If the Kaggle reviewers interpret card-name strings on a chart as a license violation, this single figure can be replaced with a chart that uses anonymised IDs (`T1, T2, …`) and a separate legend. The chart-generation code already supports this — change `data["trainer_executions"]` keys to anonymised IDs in `_make_figures.py::fig_simulator_validation`.

## Pokémon character art

- The frontend does **not** ship with any Pokémon character art assets (verified during the frontend audit; `public/icons/` is empty).
- The decklists in `FINAL_DECK.md` are pure text (PTCG Live format), no art.
- The benchmark, evaluation, and architecture figures are generated from numeric data with matplotlib defaults; no Pokémon imagery.

## Recommendation

- **SAFE to attach:** benchmarks, head_to_head, architecture, mcts_position_quality, deck_candidates.
- **CONDITIONALLY SAFE:** simulator_validation (text card names only; replace with anonymised IDs if reviewer interpretation is strict).
- **DO NOT attach:** anything from `public/icons/` (currently empty anyway), official-looking card images, screenshots that show licensed art in the browser tab/title.

No file in `submission/` contains rendered card art, character art, expansion logos, or the Pokémon trademark logo. Card-name text references are factual and standard.
